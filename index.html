What is the Austin Animal Center?


The Austin Animal Center is the municipal shelter for the city of Austin, Texas. Their mission is divided into four goals:

  1. To provide public service and a safety net for lost and homeless animals in the community by providing necessary food, water, shelter and standard municipal veterinary care for animals in need.
  2. To provide placement services that will assist lost, homeless or sheltered animals to their homes or find new homes when necessary, to provide live outcomes for at least 95% of sheltered animals.
  3. To enforce animal regulations and assist the public with animal-related concerns, including impoundment, quarantine and other rabies control services in order to protect citizens and animals in our community.
  4. To provide animal services to the public in order to educate and prevent animal homelessness and promote humane, compassionate treatment of animals and responsible pet ownership.

In connection with their mission, an enormous number of animals are still found by the shelter each year. Too many of these animals will die in shelter, and too few will find a home for adoption.

Let's take a closer look at the data to find out what's going on.

Imagine all the animals

In the charts above, we have seen what the Austin Animal Center looks like over several years. But what about on a day-to-day basis?

Every day, the shelter works tirelessly to find animals. Once found, they often stay a while in the shelter, each in their separate kennels, before they depart. Can you imagine what all of these animals look like in their kennels?

The grid on the right is stacks of animal kennels. Each cell is a real-life animal, and every second that a cell appears is ten hours that the animal actually spent inside the shelter.

Select a date to begin, then hover over the squares to see details about the animal.

When and Where are Animals Found?

Animals are found all across Austin at virtually all times of the year. However, most animals are found in the warmer spring and summer months and in downtown areas.

The Life of a Found Animal

What are the fates of all these animals once they are taken into Austin Animal Center?

Most are adopted, while many are transferred to another shelter. However, as you can see on the right, a still-too-large portion of animals will die in shelter either by euthanasia or natural causes.

Hover over the different parts of the chart to see the number of animals and corresponding outcome.

Which animals need your help the most?

Least adopted genders are Intact Female/Intact Male for Dogs and Cats, Intact Female for birds, and Unknown gender for other animals.

To help an animal in need, consider those which are not adopted most based on Gender.
