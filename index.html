What is the Austin Animal Shelter?


The Austin Animal Shelter is the municipal shelter for the city of Austin, Texas. Their mission is divided into four goals:

  1. To provide public service and a safety net for lost and homeless animals in the community by providing necessary food, water, shelter and standard municipal veterinary care for animals in need.
  2. To provide placement services that will assist lost, homeless or sheltered animals to their homes or find new homes when necessary, to provide live outcomes for at least 95% of sheltered animals.
  3. To enforce animal regulations and assist the public with animal-related concerns, including impoundment, quarantine and other rabies control services in order to protect citizens and animals in our community.
  4. To provide animal services to the public in order to educate and prevent animal homelessness and promote humane, compassionate treatment of animals and responsible pet ownership.

However, despite their best efforts, an enormous number of animals are still found by the shelter each year. Too many of these animals will die in shelter, and too few will find a home for adoption.

Imagine all the animals

In the charts above, we have seen what the Austin Animal Shelter looks like over several years. But what about on a daily basis?

Every day, the shelter works tirelessly to find animals. Once found, they often stay awhile in the shelter, each in their separate kennel, before they are sent away. Can you imagine what all of these animals look like in their kennels?

The grid to the right is several stacks of animal kennels. Each square represents an animal, and every second that the square appears is one hour that an animal spends in the shelter.

When and Where are Animals Found?

Animals are found all across Austin at virtually all times of the year.

However, most animals are found in the warmer spring and summer months and in downtown areas.

The Life of a Found Animal

What are the fates of all these animals once they are taken into the Austin Animal Shelter?

Most are adopted, while many are transferred to another shelter.

However, a still-too-large portion of animals will die in shelter either by euthanasia or natural causes.


Which pets need the most help?

Least adopted genders are Intact Female/Intact Male for Dogs and Cats, Intact Female for birds, and Unknown gender for other animals.

To help an animal in need, consider those which are not adopted most based on Gender.
